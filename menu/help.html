Help & FAQs

1. What is GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a numerical representation of a student's academic performance.

2. How do I calculate my GPA?

To calculate your GPA, enter the course name, credit hours, and marks for each course in the calculator. Then, click the "Calculate" button to see your GPA.

3. Can I add more courses to the calculator?

Yes, you can add additional courses by clicking the "Add Course" button. Enter the course details, and they will be included in your GPA calculation.

4. How accurate is the GPA calculation?

Our GPA calculator provides accurate results based on the grading scale used. However, it's essential to double-check your inputs for correctness.

5. Can I switch between dark mode and light mode?

Yes, you can toggle between dark mode and light mode using the theme switch button in the top right corner of the page.

6. What if I made a mistake in entering my course details?

You can edit or delete a course by clicking the edit (cursor) or delete (cross) icon next to it. Make sure to recalculate your GPA after any changes.

7. Is my data saved when I close the browser?

No, your data is not saved when you close the browser. It's recommended to take a screenshot or note down your GPA if needed.

8. What should I do if I encounter an error?

If you encounter an error or issue with the calculator, please contact via the "Contact Us" section for assistance.

9. Can I use this calculator for different grading scales?

Our calculator is designed for the standard 4.0 grading scale. If your institution uses a different scale, you may need to adjust your inputs accordingly.

10. Are there any mobile apps for this GPA calculator?

Currently, we do not have a dedicated mobile app. However, our website is mobile-responsive and can be used on smartphones and tablets.

11. What GPA scale does my institution use?

You can find information about your institution's GPA scale in your academic handbook or by contacting your school's registrar's office.

12. How can I improve my GPA?

To improve your GPA, focus on studying effectively, attending classes regularly, and seeking help from professors or tutors when needed. Setting realistic goals and managing your time wisely can also make a significant difference.

13. Is there a maximum number of courses I can add?

There is no maximum limit for the number of courses you can add. However, keep in mind that too many courses may make the calculator less user-friendly.

14. What is the minimum and maximum GPA possible?

The minimum GPA is 0.00, and the maximum GPA is 4.00 on our standard 4.0 grading scale. Some institutions may use different scales.

15. How often should I update my GPA?

You can update your GPA as frequently as you complete new courses or receive updated grades. It's a good practice to keep your GPA up to date for academic planning.

16. Can I print or export my GPA results?

Currently, our calculator does not have a built-in print or export feature. However, you can take a screenshot or manually record your GPA for your records.

17. Is this GPA calculator free to use?

Yes, our GPA calculator is entirely free to use. There are no hidden charges or subscriptions required.
